## Pairing map.
Let $G_1$, $G_2$ and $G_3$ be three commutative groups. Then a pairing map is a function
$$
e(·,·) : G_1 ×G_2 \rarr G_3
$$
This function takes pairs $(a_1,a_2)$ of elements from $G_1$ and $G_2$, and maps them to elements from $G_3$ such that the bilinearity property holds, which means that
for all $a_1, b_1 \in G_1$ and $a_2, b_2 \in G_2$ the following two identities are satisfied:
$$

$$
1. $e(a_1\space.\space b_1, a_2) = \overbrace{e(a_1,a_2) \space .\space e(b_1,b_2)}^{\text{operator on}\space G_3} $
2. $e(a_1,a_2\space.\space b_2) = \underbrace{e(a_1,a_2) \space .\space e(a_1,b_2)}_{\text{operator on}\space G_3}$
- Note that $a_1\space.\space b_1$ is $G_1$ group operator and $a_2\space.\space b_2$ is $G_2$ group operator.
